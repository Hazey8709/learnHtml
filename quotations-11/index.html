Quotations & Citation Elements

Tags & Descriptions

< abbr >< /abbr>: Defines an abbreviation or acronym.

< address >: Defines contact info for the author/owner of a document.

< bdo >: Defines the text direction.

< blockquote >: Defines a section that is quoted from another source.

< cite >: Defines the title of a work.

< q >: Defines a short inline quotation.

Examples

Block Quote (Blockquote Tag W/cite attribute)

Here is a quote from WWF's website:

For 60 years, WWF has worked to help people and nature thrive. As the world's leading conservation organization, WWF works in nearly 100 countries. At every level, we collaborate with people around the world to develop and deliver innovative solutions that protect communities, wildlife, and the places in which they live.

Short Quotation (q Tag)

Browsers usually insert quotation marks around the q element.

WWF's goal is to: Build a future where people live in harmony with nature.

Abbreviations (abbr Tag)

The WHO was founded in 1948.

Marking up abbreviations can give useful information to browsers, translation systems and search-engines.

Contact information (address Tag)

The HTML address element defines contact information (author/owner) of a document or article.

Written by John Doe.
Visit us at:
Example.com
Box 564, Disneyland
USA

Cite work (cite Tag)

The HTML cite element defines the title of a work. Browsers usually display cite elements in italic.

Noobie J's by Jordan. Founded in 2022.
